Universe Guide

The World of the Dova Sku Series

A spoiler-light tour of the major worlds, factions, and technologies that shape Christopher Thompkins’ life—from a quiet home on Earth to the ruins of Catora and the burning edge of Svixen space.

Ancient wars, dead empires, and a Transync network that was never meant for war. The Dova Sku universe is a place where our species is late to the party—and the bill is already due.

Worlds & Frontiers

Key Worlds

Everything starts sixty light-years from home, on a world humanity thinks it understands. It doesn’t. From Earth, the crew of the Light Skipper travels to the newly discovered exoplanet, Catora, also known as Randt to its original inhabitants.

Earth

Origin World Late Arrival

To most of its inhabitants, Earth is still a blue dot with a messy history and a shaky future. To older civilizations, it’s something else: a world that once brushed against a much larger war and then forgot. Twelve thousand years of amnesia and myth stand between what humans believe about their past and what actually happened.

  • Tech level: Pre-FTL, experimental EM Tether.
  • Conflict driver: Future Svixen invasion looms over every decision the crew makes.
  • Symbol: Home worth crossing a galaxy for.

Catora / Randt

First Contact World Forest World

A forest-covered alien world once inhabited by the Sepu, an amtillian species. Catora is humanity’s first real contact point with nonhuman intelligence—and the place where the illusion of a clean, simple colony world dies. Some of the crew choose to remain, helping build a fragile human foothold and preserve what’s left.

  • Role: Birthplace of the Dova Sku and the first real proof of the Svixen threat.
  • Theme: Survival, guilt, and rebuilding in the shadow of catastrophe.

Tual

Maakét Homeworld Stealth & Innovation

Tual is the homeworld of the Maaket, stealthy feline-like warriors whose culture blends lethal precision with rigid honor. It is a technologically advanced world, and becomes a key ally in the fight against the Svixen. Josh remains here for a time, working with Maaket engineers to develop weapons, defensive systems, and other upgrades that will keep the crew alive.

  • Notable allies: Princess Cashy, Ankir, and the Maaket leadership.
  • Specialty: Weapons systems, braiding technology, and skilled in combat.

Seutor

Tuaca and Natuul Homeworld Jungle & Desert World

An ancient, jungle- and desert-riddled planet ruled by the Tuaca, an ape-like, deeply nature-focused species, and the Natuul, and ork-like, highly tribal species. Seutor is where the Ascendant is obtained, and where a Skrema duel is fought and won to save the world from a Svixen invasion. The Tuaca ally with the humans, but walk a line between neutrality and involvement, offering wisdom and shelter rather than outright allegiance. Two Tuaca warriors join the human crew of the Ascendant as they journey to Earth.

  • Key figures: Patron Rohga, Guardian Phostop, Posis and Tivay.
  • Story role: Place of alliance, farewell, and a hard-earned hope.

Patchave

Cruillum Homeworld Desert & Resonance

Patchave is the desert world of the Cruillum, lizard-like humanoids who have turned survival into an art form. Their civilization is built on harmonic resonance and “singing to the glass,” manipulating materials and technology through precise vibrational control. The crew stops here to repair the Ascendant after a brutal space battle with the Svixen—and to use the local Transync gate to return to Seutor for parts.

  • Specialty: Resonant engineering and glass-based tech no one else fully understands.
  • Key figures: Kess.
  • Function in the story: Crucial repair yard and waypoint under pressure.
Powers & Players

Factions in the Shadows of an Old War

By the time humans step out into the wider galaxy, the sides have already been chosen. The only question is which side will use them—and which side will erase them.

Humanity

Newcomers High Variance

Human beings are late to interstellar travel, but they’re adaptable, stubborn, and bad at doing what they’re told. On Catora, that mix produces Lieutenant Christopher Thompkins: a navy SEAL with a broken past who becomes the “Dova Sku”—Hell Machine—after becoming part cyborg and binding with an ancient AI named Liam.

  • Strengths: Improvisation, emotional resilience, refusal to accept “inevitable” outcomes.
  • Weaknesses: Fragmented politics, limited tech, almost no understanding of the true rules of the game.

The Svixen

Imperial Power Extinction-Level Threat

To their enemies, the Svixen are a nightmare made of teeth, fire, and doctrine. To themselves, they’re the righteous enforcers of a brutal peace. They don’t merely conquer worlds—they correct them, pruning species and civilizations that grow in ways they consider dangerous.

  • Doctrine: Control the networks, control the species that depend on them.
  • Relationship to Earth: Historically distant, uncomfortably interested once humans leave Earth.

The Maakét of Tual

Allied Warriors Stealth Specialists

Sleek, feline, and lethally efficient, the Maakét are as much engineers as they are warriors. They specialize in weapons systems, energy shields, and ways of not being where the enemy thinks you are. Their politics are complicated, but once they commit to a fight, they commit with claws out.

  • Key allies: Princess Cashy, her sister Nessa, and the warrior Ankir.
  • Contribution: Drake Scale cloaking suits, reverse-energy concepts, and upgrades that keep the Ascendant hard to pin down.

The Tuaca of Seutor

Spiritual Warriors Neutral Ground

The Tuaca are ape-like, physically imposing, and deeply spiritual. Seutor’s deserts and jungles have taught them patience and perspective. They try to stay neutral in the wider conflict, but neutrality has limits when the Svixen bring war to their doorstep.

  • Key figures: Patron Rohga, Guardian Phostop, warriors like Posis and Tivay.
  • Contribution: The gift of the Ascendant, shielding tech, ship weapons, and a moral compass in a very amoral war.

The Natuul of Seutor

Tribal Warriors Not Friends, Not Enemies

A hulking orcish species with advanced tech but no centralized government. Tribal culture with frequent conflicts with the Tuaca. Progress comes from raiding and reverse-engineering Tuaca tech.

The Cruillum of Patchave

Resonant Engineers Harsh-World Survivors

The Cruillum are lizard-like humanoids who turned a desert world into a laboratory for harmonic technology. Their “singing to the glass” techniques let them coax weapons, shields, and structures into forms no one else can replicate. They’re not eager to pick sides, and they understand the value of keeping everyone at arm's length.

  • Contribution: Critical ship repairs, Transync gate access, and tech tuned to the Ascendant’s needs.
  • Stance: Practical, cautious, and always aware of how much heat is being spent.

The Aku’Ra & Original Uplifters of Species

Precursors Unfinished Business

Long gone—or smart enough to stay off stage—the Aku’Ra are the rumored architects behind the Transync network and numerous uplifted species. Between them, their subjects, and the Svixen, the galaxy is full of client species: cultures uplifted, drafted into a war, and then left to pick up the pieces when empires fell.

Some become allies, some enemies, and some simply refuse to pick a side—until Chris, Liam, and the Ascendant crash into their politics.

Tools & Ruins

Technology that Shouldn’t Be in Human Hands

For most of human history, the galaxy has run on systems Earth didn’t know existed. The moment Chris and Liam connect to one, everything changes.

The EM Tether

Human FTL Experimental

Earth’s first real stab at faster-than-light travel, the EM Tether is less “warp drive” and more “galactic slingshot”—a way of riding precisely shaped electromagnetic fields along precomputed paths. It’s good enough to reach Catora. It is absolutely not designed to handle the kind of traffic, interference, and weapons that older civilizations throw around as a matter of course.

The Transync Network

Ancient Infrastructure Travel & Storage

Transync is more than communication and more than transit. It’s a mesh of stations, relays, and protocols built to synchronize cities, fleets, and even entire planets. When Chris links with Liam in an old Catoran station, he taps into a faint echo of what the network used to be—and paints a target on himself in the process.

  • Capabilities: Fleet coordination, information storage, matter translation under tightly controlled rules.
  • Risks: Exploitable by empires, catastrophic failure modes on planetary scales.

Dova Sku: “Hell Machine”

Hybrid Operator Living Weapon

“Dova Sku” is an alien phrase that roughly translates to “Hell Machine”—a battlefield role where a cybernetic soldier becomes a terror in battle. In Chris’s case, that also comes with an alien AI named Liam, and the merge is anything but clean. The result is faster reaction times, deeper situational awareness, and a man who’s never fully alone in his own head again.

  • Upside: One person can fight like a squadron.
  • Downside: The line between “tool” and “person” gets blurry on both sides.

The Ascendant

Captured Warship Moving Fault Line

The Ascendant is more than a ship—it’s a mobile embassy, weapons platform, and cultural pressure cooker. Human survivors, Maakét weapons tech, Tuaca shielding, and Cruillum-tuned systems all share the same hull while trying not to kill each other before the Svixen do.

Everywhere it goes, it drags the past with it: old treaties, old grudges, and technologies that were never meant to merge.

Big Picture

High-Level Timeline (Spoiler-Light)

This isn’t a full canon timeline, just enough to understand where the series sits relative to older wars and future trouble.

Long Before Human History
Rise of the Aku’Ra & Millenium Wars

The Aku’Ra built the Transync network, uplifted client species, and fought wars over who gets to shape the galaxy. The Svixen emerge as one of several powers willing to burn worlds to enforce their version of order.

Prehistoric Earth
A Brush with Something Bigger

Earth isn’t entirely ignored. Whether as a test case, a mistake, or something stranger, humanity brushes against the wider conflict. Whatever happened, it’s buried under myth, ice, and twelve thousand years of forgetting.

Near Future
Discovery of Catora & the EM Tether

Humanity finds Catora and builds the EM Tether to reach it. The mission that carries Chris, Josh, and the rest of the crew to this “new beginning” unknowingly lights up an old node in the Transync network.

Transync
Contact, Ruins, and the First Fall

The crew of the Light Skipper lands on Catora,discovers old ruins, alien creatures, and the first hints of the Transync network. The Svixen answer the signal in force. Chris becomes Dova Sku in a last stand that will define who survives to tell anyone what happened.

Ascendant
A Warship and a Way Home

The survivors take control of the Ascendant and try to fight their way back toward Earth through a galaxy already scarred by Svixen campaigns. Old alliances, broken empires, and new enemies all converge around one uncomfortable fact: if Earth doesn’t know what’s coming, it won’t have a say in what happens next.

Book 3 & Beyond
Secrets of the Network

The final movements of the Dova Sku saga push deeper into the Transync network and the truth of why any of this was built in the first place. Some wars end. Others just change shape.

This page is meant as a companion to the books, not a replacement—major twists, character fates, and specific battles still live in the novels.

Quick Reference

Glossary

A fast-reference list for the names and terms that come up most often in the Dova Sku universe.

Dova Sku – Alien term roughly translated as “Hell Machine.” A hybrid operator role where a cyborg soldier is able to overcome the odds. In Svixen history, their warriors are supposed to only be able to be killed by a soldier of hell. The Hell Machine in Svixen history was a Svixen warrior who completely eradicated a competing tribe single-handedly.
Liam – A sentient station AI originally bound to a Transync station in Catora’s infrastructure. After a catastrophic merge event, he lives inside Chris’s mind as both ally and constant companion.
Transync – A galaxy-spanning network system used for travel, coordination, communication, and matter translation. Older than human civilization and not built for Svixen use.
EM Tether – Earth’s experimental faster-than-light travel method, used to send the first mission to Catora.
Maakét – Feline-like warriors from Tual. Experts in weapons, shield tech, and precision warfare.
Tuaca – Ape-like, spiritual warriors from Seutor, known for honor, ritual, and a fierce sense of balance.
Cruillum – Lizard-like humanoids from Patchave who use harmonic resonance and “singing to the glass” to shape advanced technology.
Svixen – A powerful, empire-scale species whose “peacekeeping” looks a lot like extermination from the outside.
Aku’Ra – A precursor civilization associated with uplifting programs, Transync infrastructure, and a long trail of morally questionable decisions.
Light Skipper – The ship that carries humanity’s first Catora expedition.
Ascendant – A secured civilian ship turned warship, lifeboat, embassy, and battering ram as the survivors push back toward Earth.
